    Frequently asked questions

    If you have a quick question, check to see if there's already a quick answer!

    My computer is slow and keeps crashing, why?

    There are several reasons why a computer may just stop working or "freeze". Excessive background processes, programs running in the background, overwork your RAM. High performance games or programs that take up more RAM than you have available could slow down your desktop. A computer that starts to slow down can be overloaded with temporary data or programs using up its memory. Freezing can also be caused by malware or errors on your hard disk.

    My monitor isn't loading anything, what do I do?

    The easiest solution is to make sure the monitor is on. If the power light isn't on, press the power button and hold. If that doesn't work, unplug the monitor from the desktop and wait, then reconnect. Make sure that the monitor is connected properly to the back of the computer. If you stepped away from the computer for a bit and returned to a black screen, the computer is likely asleep. Try moving your mouse, clicking the mouse buttons, or pressing any key (e.g., spacebar) to wake it up. If none of those actions work, press the power button.

    My PC frequently loses connection to the wireless network.

    Right-click the wireless connection icon in the notification area, then select Troubleshoot problems. It’s possible that your location may be causing interference with the WiFi signal – try moving to another location to attempt to connect to the service. If you find that the wireless connection is still weak, consider purchasing a different antenna for your router or set up another router as a signal repeater. If your network includes a wireless route or wireless modem/router combination, unplug the power cord.

    I keep getting a security pop-up when trying to connect to my WIFI.

    Public WiFi services trigger a "safety" on some devices automatically to increase the security settings to protect you. It's important that you keep your device protected on public networks. To do that, try to install an software and a firewall on your laptop as a precaution. Most importantly, don't enter any sensitive details on public netowrks. Make sure you are aware of the risks of connecting to public networks before you do so and take the necessary steps to keep yourself protected.

    How do I set up my browser to accept cookies?

    Do you have to accept cookies? No, there's nothing forcing you. Blocking all cookies limit the speed of your Internet experience. Cookies are files with small bits of data used to speed up your interaction with a website when you use more than once. Websites that use these cookies are always required to ask you for cookies. If you want, you can go to your browser settings and go to privacy settings to change the way websites ask you for cookies and make it automatic.
